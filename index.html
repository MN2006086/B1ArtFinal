La Liberté guidant le peuple by Eugène Delacroix (1830)

English Version

A bold and revolutionary masterpiece of Romanticism, La Liberté guidant le peuple (Liberty Leading the People) by Eugène Delacroix captures the spirit of the July Revolution of 1830 in France. The painting depicts a powerful allegory of Liberty, personified as a woman leading revolutionaries over the barricades. The dynamic composition, dramatic lighting, and raw emotion embody the ideals of freedom and resistance. My absolute personal favorite of all time. Explore the very foundations of the French revolution and current culture, along with the values of Liberté, Egalité, Fraternité. Learn about history, Lady Liberty, why a lady was used, and connect to other social revolutions (like the Soviets with Marxism!) and imagine yourself in such an extraordinary moment. Use passé composé and imparfait to discuss the historical context, and analyse her movement, emotion and symbol in Romantic art.

Characteristics: Revolutionary Spirit, Dramatic Composition, Symbolism of Liberté, Egalité, Fraternité

Skills Improved: Reading, Writing, Speaking, Listening, Cultural Awareness

Rue de Paris, temps de pluie by Gustave Caillebotte (1877)

A striking Impressionist/Realist painting depicting a rainy day in Paris, with elegantly dressed pedestrians and reflections on wet cobblestone streets. Caillebotte’s precise perspective and urban realism make it a fascinating piece to analyze. Describe this painting with urban vocabulary, atmosphere, emotion and perspective, and learn about 19th-century Parisian life! As a fun exercise, try find the modern photo of where this is in Paris today, and compare how they have changed! Then, imagine how it will look in the future, practising your past, present and future tense!

Characteristics: Detailed cityscape with a vanishing point perspective. Soft, natural light reflecting off wet streets. Everyday life in 19th-century Paris captured with realism.

Skills Improved: Reading, Writing, Cultural Awareness

La Persistance de la Mémoire by Salvador Dalí (1931)

One of the most famous Surrealist paintings, La Persistance de la Mémoire (The Persistence of Memory) is known for its melting clocks, dreamlike landscape, and eerie stillness. While Dalí was Spanish, this painting had a major influence on French Surrealism, a movement deeply connected to France. Describe its abstract concepts and emotions, use adjectives for texture, shape, and mood, and practice talking about time and memory in French. Explain and attempt to articulate the philosophical concepts with time and dreams. Elaborate on what time means to you, and what it could have meant to Dalí. (Hint: Look at the painting's name!)

Characteristics: Surrealist imagery with dreamlike distortion, Melting clocks symbolizing time and relativity, Mysterious and almost hallucinatory atmosphere

Skills Improved: Writing, Speaking, Cultural Awareness
